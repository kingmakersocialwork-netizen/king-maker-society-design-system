DM Scripts
💬

Cold Outreach

First message to someone who doesn't know us yet. Keep it short, genuine, and about them.

Yo [Name], came across your page and I fw the content. You training [art]? Asking because we work with a lot of fighters who are trying to grow their brand and stay accountable. Might be something worth looking into depending on what you're working on.

🔥

Warm Lead (Follows / Likes)

They engaged with the page. They already know something about us. Lead with that.

Hey [Name], appreciate the follow. What martial art do you train? Just curious what you're working toward right now.

🏆

Comment Trigger ("KING")

They commented KING on a post. Hit them right away while the energy is there.

Saw you dropped "KING" on the post. Appreciate that. What are you training right now? Trying to get a feel for where you're at before I send you anything.

📋

Questionnaire Send

Use this after you've built some context on who they are and what they want.

Cool, this actually makes a lot of sense based on what you said. Before I go into detail I want to make sure I point you in the right direction. I have a quick form that takes 2 minutes. It helps us understand where you're at so we're not just throwing stuff at you. [Questionnaire Link] Fill that out and I'll follow up personally based on your answers.

💰

Payment Link Send

After they're sold. Keep it clean — no over-explaining.

Based on everything you told me, the [Basic / VIP] membership is the right move for where you're at. It's $[30/100]/month, cancel anytime, no contracts. Here's the link: [Payment Link] Once you're in I'll personally make sure you get properly set up and introduced to the group.

🔄

Follow-Up (No Response)

They went quiet. One clean follow-up. Don't chase — just check in.

Hey just circling back. No pressure at all. If the timing isn't right that's fully cool. Just didn't want to leave you hanging if you had questions. Either way keep training.

Objection Handling
💰

"Can't Afford It"

Don't argue. Acknowledge it. Then reframe the value.

I get that. $30 has to make sense for where you're at. What I'll say is this — most guys who join end up saying the accountability alone is worth it. It's not just information, it's an environment. But if timing isn't right, I'm not going to push you. When does it make more sense for you?

🤔

"I Need To Think About It"

Don't disappear. Ask a real question to find out what's actually holding them back.

Totally fair. What part are you weighing up? Is it the timing, the price, or just not sure if it's the right fit? Asking because I want to give you an honest answer, not just sell you on it.

❓

"What Even Is This?"

They don't know the brand. Give a clean, direct explanation.

King Maker Society is an online self-improvement brotherhood for martial artists. It's a paid community where fighters hold each other accountable, share wins, and work on their mindset, discipline, and personal brand together. We run live sessions twice a week and the group chat is active every single day. It's $30/month. No contracts, cancel anytime.

Lead Flow SOP
📈

Standard Lead Flow

Follow this sequence for every new lead. Don't skip steps.

  • 1Start the conversation naturally. Ask about their martial art, what they're working on, where they train. Listen more than you talk.
  • 2Find their main pain point. Discipline? Accountability? Content? Exposure? Identify it before you pitch anything.
  • 3Drop a little value. Say something useful based on what they told you. Builds trust before the ask.
  • 4Send the questionnaire. Use the questionnaire script. Wait for them to fill it out before pitching.
  • 5Personalize the pitch. Use their answers. Speak directly to what they said. Don't use a generic pitch.
  • 6Handle objections calmly. Don't pressure. If they're not ready, set a follow-up date and move on.
  • 7Send the payment link. Once they're convinced. Keep the message short. Don't over-explain at this stage.
  • 8Log the lead. Update their status in the CRM. Mark next follow-up date if not converted.

Weekly Content Workflow
📅

Weekly Content SOP

Run this every week. Consistency is the standard — no excuses.

  • 1Sunday: Plan your content for the week. Decide what you are posting on which days. No winging it.
  • 2Monday: Film or create your first piece of content. If you filmed over the weekend, begin editing.
  • 3Tuesday/Wednesday: Post your first reel or piece of content. Write the caption using the template below.
  • 4Thursday: Engage on King's Media and King Maker Society pages. Comment, share, and support other team members' content.
  • 5Friday: Post your second piece for the week OR a story/engagement post. Screenshot any wins or highlights.
  • 66Saturday: Attend and participate in the King Maker Society live session at 2PM CST.
  • 7End of week: Report your content output, reach, and any leads generated to the team.
